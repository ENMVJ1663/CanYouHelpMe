Common myths and misconceptions

“You are not your illness. You have an individual story to tell. You have a name, a history, a personality. Staying yourself is part of the battle.” — Julian Seifter

'People with mental illness are poor and or less intelligent'

When one have too many things on their mind, it can be diffcult to focus on their surroundings.

This can affect one to be unable to understand things easily, because they are easily distracted.

However, this does not mean that they are stupid or less intelligent. They are also just overwhelmed on their own thoughts.

'People with mental illness are dangerous and violent'

There are times where one may feel extremely overwhelmed with their own thought, especially when one are trying to understand the reason people hurl hurtful comments to them.

Hence, one can be irritated or feel sad towards the hurtful comments that others may have gave.

In turn, this may cause them to appear dangerous and violent. But, they may just be trying to arrange their thoughts, and not to be overwhelmed.

As such, one could take a few deep breath, and relax theirselves.

'Mental illness are uncommon'

That is not true, as over 970 million people in the world are alreadly affected by a mental disorder.

As such, you don't have to be afarid, or even feel alone. Many others have experienced similar pain to you.

'People who have mental disorder are crazy'

Mental illnesses are the result of poor neurotransmitter activity. In which, this may cause all the messages that are supposed to be being sent to the brain completely off.

In turn, this may cause one to act and think differently, and hence act in a way different than the norm. However, acting differently than the norm, are usually defined as special, weird, or even worse, crazy.

You are acting in a way natural to you, and hence, there is no need to mind what others say to you. You are special in your own way.
